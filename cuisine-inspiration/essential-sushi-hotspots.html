11 Essential Sushi spots to order from

  • 18 August 2017
  • Dylan Newe
  • Read time: 4 min
sushi lunch dinner japanese dublin limerick cork galway
Share this article

Who hasn't had an intense craving for sushi? Healthy, fresh and cheap, it's addictive fuel for an intensely busy life and along with other yummy Japanese food, one of the most enjoyed dishes around the world. You simply can't beat the range of colours, textures and tastes in each sushi dish, not to mention how every roll is a beautiful work of art in its own right. So whether it's nigiri, unagi or any of the exciting other varieties we've found 11 of your ultimate sushi favourites you can have delivered straight to your door with Deliveroo anytime.

Wa Café

A post shared by WA CAFE • Galway (@wacafegalway) on Mar 21, 2016 at 4:55am PDT

One of The Irish Times Best Places to Eat 2017, this sparkling gem of the Galway culinary scene delivers hand-crafted sushi masterpieces that are truly Instagram-worthy. We love their take on the classic sashimi plate and if you're feeling adventurous or feeding a group try the epic 42-piece evening sushi platter.

Where: Galway 

Wasabi

A post shared by Wasabi Sushi Bar (@wasabisushibardublin) on Mar 15, 2017 at 7:10pm PDT

Serving up an extensive selection of delightful sushi choices, Wasabi is ideal for those who like a little of everything. There's a dish here to suit all tastes from the trendy salmon avocado roll to the hand-rolled temaki. You can even indulge with a special banana & nutella sushi treat for dessert.

Where: Dublin 

Kuraudo

A post shared by Gregory Manley (@gregorylmanley) on Jun 26, 2017 at 11:08am PDT

The cosy interiors of this hidden spot belie what's regularly described as "the best sushi in Dublin" by local food lovers. You can't go wrong with any of the awesome selection on offer here but our secret pick is the slightly spicy shimo.

Where: Dublin 

Aoki Sushi

A post shared by @dublinbites (@dublinbites) on Oct 19, 2016 at 2:45am PDT

Aoki's traditional Japanese approach and easy-to-choose menu dictates that you'll find plenty to adore here. Their chef's special Rainbow Roll is not just a rare visual delight but the perfect sushi introduction, vivid pops of colour from the sashimi and avocado matching the flavour packed freshness.

Where: Dublin

Kyoto Sushi and Noodles

A post shared by Emily_cooks_n_eats (@emily_cooks_n_eats) on Aug 10, 2017 at 2:33am PDT

Named after the colourful Japanese city, Kyoto Sushi is a world of authentic Asian tastes using the freshest fish. Variety is the spice of life after all and we can't get enough of their Kyoto Platter. With six slices of sashimi, six pieces of nigiri and eight pieces of norimaki, it's the perfect way to ensure you don't have sushi envy at the end!

Where: Limerick 

Dashi Sushi

A post shared by Dashi Deli Sushi & Noodle Bar (@dashisushicork) on Jul 21, 2017 at 2:26pm PDT

Sushi isn't just one of the tastiest foods but also one of the healthiest with different types of fish containing high levels of vitamins, minerals and omega-3's, meaning Dashi Sushi's menu is not just delicious but nutritious too. You'll find an excellent array of futomaki, chumaki and lots of other tempting sushi types here to fill you up with.

Where: Cork 

Banyi Japanese Dining

Serving up a huge menu of sushi dishes, Banyi is a firm Dublin favourite among Japanese cuisine lovers and boasts some of the most magical food presentation this side of Tokyo. There are separate sections for all types of their stunning sushi from temaki to gunkan maki so all you need to do is decide what to order.

Where: Dublin 

Eatokyo

A post shared by Alina Chervyachuck (@alinya_) on Jul 26, 2017 at 10:52am PDT

A local treasure among Dublin's foodie bloggers, Eatokyo's varied sushi combinations and rolls are revered for their winning flavours and freshness. Our pick is the soft shell crab tempura futomaki which are a must for any seafood fan.

Where: Dublin 

Taikichi

A post shared by Elizabeth Barrett (@liibbybee) on Jul 7, 2016 at 5:54am PDT

Taikichi dish out a range of Japanese favourites every day but it's the sushi that has locals going back for more. Try their norimaki special dragon roll with deep fried jumbo tiger prawn and avocado wasabi sauce for a dish that packs a flavour punch.

Where: Limerick 

Maki Sushi Rolls

A post shared by Sharon Joy (@sharn_joy) on Apr 27, 2016 at 5:53am PDT

Sometimes simplicity is key and Maki's compact menu of handrolls is perfect for the sushi fanatic who knows what they want. For a local twist on this wonderful Japanese staple, you can go with the lemon and lime cured organic Irish salmon rolled with chives, cucumber and rocket leaves.

Where: Cork

Fujiyama

A post shared by Alex (@alexkatefw) on Apr 1, 2017 at 11:28am PDT

A gem located right in the heart of Dublin's bustling O'Connell Street, Fujiyama has the widest selection of sushi you could ask for. The sushi bento box is our favourite and each dish is an explosion of gorgeous colours and flavours.

Where: Dublin
