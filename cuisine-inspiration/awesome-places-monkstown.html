4 awesome places that deliver to Monkstown

  • 1 July 2017
  • Editor
  • Read time: 3 min
cajun monkstown italian beer asian noodles
Share this article

It's not just the centre of Dublin that's buzzing on the foodie front. Monkstown, Blackrock and Dún Laoghaire have plenty of cracking places for a bite or a beer. We're rounded up a few of our favourites for you to check out for your next takeaway.

1. Rage Restaurant

A post shared by Rochelle Moriarty (@rage_restaurant) on Dec 19, 2016 at 1:52pm PST

Blackrock is the setting for this flame-grilled specialist with plenty of deep south U.S. of A. vibes. Check out their signature Cajun-spiced chicken breast, it's been given the smoky flame treatment and served with rice and a zingy Cajun sauce.

If you're in the mood for surf and turf, try chargrilled black tiger prawn skewers and a burger combination. Nothing beats a prime beef burger licked by fire, and Rage turns up the heat with their mighty Smoky Stack – finished with smoky bacon, tobacco onions, smoked cheese and Jameson glaze.

Where: Rage Restaurant, Blackrock

2. Dunne & Crescenzi

A post shared by Dunne and Crescenzi (@dunneandcrescenzi) on Nov 1, 2014 at 7:44am PDT

It all began with a little enoteca in Sutton opened by husband and wife team Eileen Dunne and Stefano Crescenzi. Fast forward 20 years and they run a much loved Irish-Italian food empire with half a dozen restaurants and cafés, plus a grocery shop and cookbook.

Their Blackrock venue follows the winning blueprint – simple Italian food using the best ingredients and a good dose of Mediterranean passion. From enticing antipasti and fresh pastas to glorious grills and proper pizzas using 24-hour risen dough, it's a menu of our favourite Italian dishes prepared with extra love.

Where: Dunne & Crescenzi, Blackrock

3. The Dark Horse

A post shared by Alan Daly (@garthicus) on Mar 3, 2017 at 8:00am PST

Run by the crafty folk at Galway Bay Brewery, the Dark Horse is getting things rocking in Blackrock. This specialist beer bar brims with hand crafted and small batch brews. For full-on flavour, check out their own core range: the chocolate milk stout Buried at Sea, classic IPA Althea, the red toned Bay Ale and the fruity Full Sail.

And if you feel like nibbles with your beer, they've only got one choice but it's pure perfection: a kilo bucket of chicken wings, doused in their own 'Of Foam and Fury' hot sauce. Tasty beers and a bucket of wings – what more could anyone possibly need?

Where: The Dark Horse, Blackrock

4. Kyoto Asian Street Food

A post shared by feedmefuel (@feedmefuel) on Jul 7, 2017 at 11:27am PDT

Sea, sand, the heady aromas from sizzling woks wafting through the air – and sometimes ever a bit of sun – who needs Thailand when you've got Kyoto Asian Street Food in Dún Laoghaire? Save yourself the plane tickets and grab a taste of authentic Thai cuisine on your doorstep.

From hot and sour soups to various curries, noodle dishes and wok specials, Kyoto's offerings are bursting with aromatic spices and fresh Thai flavours. Or feel like snacking on street food? You'll find a feast of skewers, rolls, wings and ribs – and they do fantastic dumplings, too.

Where: Kyoto Asian Street Food, Dún Laoghaire
