11 of the Best Burgers in Dublin

  • 23 August 2017
  • Dylan Newe
  • Read time: 4 min
burgers meat dublin irish food
Share this article

For some, the ultimate test of a thriving cosmopolitan city is the quality of its burger offering! So it's been exciting to see the explosion of top quality, affordable and varied burger joints right across the capital in recent years.

Whether it's taking cues from cities like New York on how to duplicate their delicious cheeseburgers or uniquely putting a fresh spin on renowned Irish classics (you won't know you needed Clonakilty black pudding in your burger until you've had it), you absolutely won't be stuck for choice for the primest, juiciest burger options right across Dublin.

So here's our hot list of the best burgers right now in Dublin, all on Deliveroo.

1. Pitt Bros

A post shared by Pitt Bros. BBQ (@pittbrosbbq) on Dec 5, 2016 at 1:23am PST

Roo Pick: The sumptuous Buttermilk Bird is the burger equivalent of a skyscraper. Alongside a crispy battered & marinated chicken breast is house pickled slaw, hot sauce and chipotle mayo. Are you up for the challenge?

Where: Georges Street, Millennium Walkway.

2. The Counter

A post shared by Dublin Foodguide #dublinfood (@dublinfoodguide) on Jul 13, 2017 at 10:30am PDT

Roo Pick: The Counter's beastly burgers are famous across the city for their staggeringly crammed creations. Try The Bacon BBQ which adds smoked bacon, fried onion strings and hickory bbq to an already juicy burger.

Where: Dundrum 

3. Jo'Burger

A post shared by Kelly Henderson (@turnedup2eleven) on Aug 2, 2017 at 1:40pm PDT

Roo Pick: The Ikqezi combines your choice of tantalising burger preference (from the choice of Irish beef, lamb, chicken, fish or veggie) with caramelised chilli banana, bacon & goat's cheese, mayo, lettuce, tomato and red onion. Are you drooling yet?!

Where: Smithfield.

4. Bobo's

A post shared by Menja't Barcelona (@menjatbarcelona) on Aug 15, 2017 at 4:00am PDT

Roo Pick: Outrageous burgers don't just have to mean meat, especially with a mammoth prospect like this. The Veggie Dame teams home-made falafel with tzatziki sauce, roasted peppers, red onion, tomato, iceberg lettuce, hot salsa & crème fraîche. You might need to eat this one in private!

Where: Abbey Street, Dame Street, Wexford Street.

5. Farmer Browns

Roo Pick: Keeping with the farm theme, try the Massey Ferguson - it's two 4oz beef patties, melted cheddar, streaky bacon and an onion ring served with beef tomato, aioli, lettuce and cola BBQ sauce!! You absolutely need this!

Where: Rathmines, Sandymount

6. Gourmet Burger Kitchen

Roo Pick: Has to be The Don - this kingpin of the burger family delivers onion jam, baconnaise, American cheese and gorgonzola, pickled onion rings and mayo all inside a yummy brioche bun! Respect the master.

Where: South Anne Street, South William Street, Temple Bar, Swords.

7. The Chili Shack

Roo Pick: Dublin's newest pretender to the burger throne, you have to tuck into the Chili Cheese Burger at this Dublin 7 hotspot. Jalapeno chilies with melted cheese, need we say anymore?!

Where: Stoneybatter

8. On the Pig's Back

A post shared by A V R I L ⚡️ (@dublincitymum) on Oct 8, 2016 at 1:12pm PDT

Roo Pick: You really can't go wrong with the Irish Pride! 6oz Irish beef, cheddar, bacon and Ballymaloe relish all inside a soft bun. It's utterly irresistible!

Where: Capel Street

9. Eddie Rocket's

Roo Pick: The outrageous Bacon & Egg Clonakilty burger puts a distinctive Irish twist on the genre, served with both a slice of Clonakilty Black pudding & White pudding and topped with a fried egg for the full irresistible Burger / Fry Up combo! It's as Irish as the Angelus or Marty Whelan!

Where: Swords, Donnybrook, Stillorgan, O'Connell Street, Dame Street, Wexford Street, Blackrock, Phibsboro, Rathmines, Parnell Street.

10. Real Gourmet Burger

Roo Pick: If you're ordering a whopper burger you might as well go full whack and nothing beats The Holy Moly for sheer impact. Roasted peppers, melted Swiss cheese, chili relish, jalapenos and a side of sour cream.

Where: Terenure 

8. Platform 61

A post shared by Dublin Foodguide #dublinfood (@dublinfoodguide) on Dec 25, 2016 at 10:30am PST

Roo Pick: The Platform 61 Burger is a honking slice of beef complete with smoked Irish cheese and homemade spicy relish. Takes tasty burgers to the next level!

Where: South William Street

No matter how high you want it stacked, get your mouth-watering burger fix on Deliveroo now.
