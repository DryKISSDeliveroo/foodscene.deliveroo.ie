Bank holiday: 10 perfect food and craft beer pairings

  • 4 August 2017
  • Dylan Newe
  • Read time: 5 min
craft beer alcohol weekend food dublin cork galway
Share this article

Summer bank holidays are about relaxation, catching up and kicking back with some great food and a refreshing ale while the weather's still warm enough. And with so many excellent, award-winning breweries right here in Ireland it's never been such a good time for craft beers. We've put together some excellent food pairings for some of the best local brews (along with a few international favourites) all of which are available on Deliveroo.

Brewdog

A post shared by Baggot Street Wines (@baggotstreetwines) on May 7, 2017 at 12:46pm PDT

Fresh and full of flavour, the globally-renowned Brewdog are a craft beer phenomenon and their Punk IPA reveals an explosion of tropical full flavours with citrus and pine while Dead Pony matches floral and spicy undertones for a zesty lemon aroma. Pair both with either a whopper filling-packed burger or any irresistible spicy dish (curries are good!)

Where: Baggot Street Wines, The Wine Barrel, Drinkstore, Martin's Off-Licence

Howling Gale

A post shared by Eight Degrees Brewing (@eightdegreesbrewing) on Nov 4, 2015 at 12:07pm PST

Created by Eight Degrees Brewing in Mitchelstown, Co.Cork, Howling Gale is the brewery's first and most popular beer, balancing pale golden colour with pleasant grapefruit and citrus aroma. For the ideal food pairing, you can't go wrong with the salty charm of fish and chips or a smoked salmon dish where the citrus notes act like a squeeze of lemon.

Where: Baggot Street Wines (Dublin), Martin's Off-Licence (Dublin), Crackbird (Dublin), Riba (Dublin), Uncle Pete's (Cork), The Pie Maker (Galway) 

Bay Ale

A post shared by Anna Harjulehto Bollmann (@anna_bollmann) on May 6, 2017 at 1:37am PDT

Galway Bay Brewery's selection of Irish hand-crafted, thirst-quenching craft beers will suit any taste but this Red Ale strikes a particularly smooth and balanced flavour. This Bay Ale goes with most food types, but for those summer nights especially salad, cold meats and cheeses!

Where: Galway Bay Brewery (Against the Grain), Galway Bay Brewery (Brew Dock), Galway Bay Brewery (The 108), Galway Bay Brewery (The Beer Market), Galway Bay Brewery (The Gasworks Bar), Galway Bay Brewery (The Dark Horse), Galway Bay Brewery (Beer Traders), Galway Bay Brewery - Galway (Salt House), Galway Bay Brewery - Galway (Oslo Gastro Bar).

Chieftain Pale Ale

A post shared by Franciscan Well (@franciscanwellbrewery) on Jun 20, 2017 at 3:13am PDT

Cork's own Franciscan Well brewery are behind this gorgeously high-hopped Irish IPA which delivers a rich appealing balance of citrus aroma and medium bitterness. For the perfect food accompaniment, tuck into a plate of sizzling fried chicken or order a delicate blue cheese.

Where: Franciscan Well

Brooklyn Lager

An American classic which is refreshingly smooth, the award-winning Brooklyn Lager is fresh and flowery. A wonderful complement to warm summer days, a glass of this goes superbly well with any rip-roaring pizzas, burgers or tender smoked meats.

Where: My Meat Wagon 

Founders All Day

A post shared by Founders Brewing Co. (@foundersbrewing) on Jun 21, 2017 at 10:47am PDT

Naturally brewed, Founders IPA is a global favourite for craft beer enthusiasts who enjoy the clean finish and aromatic quality. Like most IPA's, you can team this with any arrestingly tasty burger of your choice but this will also taste fantastic alongside spicy food or fried shrimp.  

Where to find it: Drinkstore 

Full Sail

A post shared by Galway Bay Brewery (@galway_bay_brewery) on Jul 26, 2017 at 2:11am PDT

Delving again into the varied Galway Bay Brewery range, Full Sail has a 5.8% ABV which carries the flavour and hops right through to the taste. The acidity and bitterness of this IPA makes it ideal for raging hot Mexican and Indian dishes - think of spicy chillies and curries.  

Where to find it: Galway Bay Brewery (Against the Grain), Galway Bay Brewery (Brew Dock), Galway Bay Brewery (The 108), Galway Bay Brewery (The Beer Market), Galway Bay Brewery (The Gasworks Bar), Galway Bay Brewery (The Dark Horse), Galway Bay Brewery (Beer Traders), Galway Bay Brewery - Galway (Salt House), Galway Bay Brewery - Galway (Oslo Gastro Bar).

The White Hag Bran & Sceolan

A post shared by The White Hag Brewing Co. (@thewhitehag) on Feb 13, 2016 at 5:57am PST

Named after two mythical wolfhounds of Fionn Mac Cumhaill, this homegrown IPA from Co.Sligo is deliciously fruity with aromas of grapefruit and mango. If you're a fan, a creamy Irish blue cheese would be just right with this one. If you're not quite that adventurous, any Vietnamese dish is equally delicious.

Where to find it: Baggot Street Wines, The Wine Barrel, The Fresh Kitchen, Drinkstore

Wicklow Wolf IPA

A post shared by Wicklow Wolf Brewing Company (@wicklowwolf) on Jul 16, 2017 at 6:03am PDT

An Irish brewery gaining worldwide accolades, Wicklow Wolf's IPA is a classic NorthWest American-style IPA with hints of citrus, pine and passionfruit. To rock your tastebuds even more, dish it up alongside some succulent BBQ or a sensational bowl of pasta.

Where to find it: Gutterball, The Wine Barrel

Knockmealdown Irish Stout

A post shared by Eight Degrees Brewing (@eightdegreesbrewing) on Nov 3, 2016 at 2:07am PDT

Named after the often-windswept Munster mountains, this robust Irish stout made by 8 Degrees brewing offers a deep chocolatey aroma with distinct espresso flavours and won a gold star at the Great Taste Awards. Try it with a warm hearty Irish stew or alongside a richly indulgent chocolate cake.

Where to find it: Martin's Off-Licence
