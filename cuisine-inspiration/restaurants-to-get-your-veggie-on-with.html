6 restaurants to get your tasty veggie on with

  • 28 September 2017
  • Dylan Newe
  • Read time: 3 min
vegetarian dinner lunch galway cork limerick galway
Share this article

From eye-popping, colourful salads to inventive yet hearty mains, vegetarian food has never been as accessible or as exciting to eat. With fresh ideas and even fresher ingredients, it's truly time to ditch the meat (at least every now and then) and embrace the veggie. Here are some of the ultimate vegetarian dishes you can order right across the Irish cities now right here on Deliveroo.

Sprout & Co

A post shared by J i l l W a t s o n (@jillwatsonxo) on Feb 13, 2017 at 7:13am PST

Sprout's use of seasonal local ingredients to create their unmistakably flavourful lunch choices has quickly found a cult following across Dublin. Veggie highlights include the Super Guacabowle and the New Falafel Salad while their breakfast menu delights with cold pressed juices and hot porridge bowls.

Where: Ballsbridge (Breakfast), Dawson Street (Breakfast), Lower Mount Street (Breakfast), Ballsbridge (Lunch), Dawson Street (Lunch), Lower Mount Street (Lunch)

Xian Street Food

A post shared by 1lady1adventure (@1lady1adventure) on Sep 7, 2017 at 6:54am PDT

Bringing the tastes of Asian street food to the city of the tribes, Xian's vegan salads are a delicious assortment of green goodness, packed with wonderful revitalising produce such as bean sprouts, rice noodles and soybeans. Fresh, quick and most importantly, incredibly tasty.

Where: Galway 

The Buttery

A post shared by Jody O'Sullivan (@chefjodyosullivan) on Aug 19, 2016 at 11:34am PDT

Limerick's hippest brunch spot is just as generous when it comes to the veggies with a host of tantalising dishes across their extensive menu. Try the halloumi and pineapple burger with red pepper hummus for a brunchtime thrill or fork up for the clean green curry to feel as good inside as out.

Where: Limerick 

Cornucopia

A post shared by Lily Joyce (@latest_lil) on Sep 7, 2017 at 12:41pm PDT

One of Dublin's longest running and most established vegetarian hotspots, Cornucopia scarcely needs an introduction but for those not in the know (yet), what you'll find is a tasty array of yummy salads, soups, tarts and sweet bites with a lean on all that's good and nutritious.

Where: Dublin 

Cocu

A post shared by Claire (@clairejup_) on Jun 26, 2017 at 4:58am PDT

Cocu's food revolution has taken Dublin by storm and their thoughtful, worldly food concepts encompass meat-free dishes including the sumptuous Bombay Bowl with chickpeas, cauliflower and pomegranate or the reviving Buddha Bowl which marries kale, quinoa and goji berries.

Where: Baggot Street (Breakfast), Baggot Street (Lunch), Hatch Street (Breakfast), Hatch Street (Lunch)

Good to Go
