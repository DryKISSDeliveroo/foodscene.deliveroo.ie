5 healthy lunch choices in Dublin

  • 3 June 2017
  • Editor
  • Read time: 3 min
lunch dublin healthy eating pad thai soup
Share this article

Who said healthy has to be boring? We show you some of the most filling, fresh and feisty meals on Deliveroo in Dublin that pack in just as much nutrition as they do flavour. You can thank us later.

1. Cinco De Fryo Box, Little Ass Burrito Bar

A post shared by Little Ass Burrito Bar (@littleassdub) on Sep 15, 2016 at 6:42am PDT

Fresh fillings, zingy flavours and customisable up the wazoo, burritos are perfect for lunch. We love the Cinco De Fryo for a treat that'll set us up for the afternoon, with lime and chilli marinated chargrilled chicken, sour cream, fresh coriander and roasted sweet potato. Drop the carby tortilla wrap and get all the goodness served up in a box for a really virtuous dinner.

Where: Little Ass Burrito Bar, Dublin

2. Prawn Pad Thai, Cocu

A post shared by CoCu (@cocu_kitchen) on Mar 29, 2017 at 7:31am PDT

Wholesome food doesn't need to be based on a bed of leaves. This hearty pad thai dish proves that point. Packing a shed-load of flavour, this pad thai uses sesame rice noodles as its base, topped with juicy garlic prawns, and stir-fried with radish, spring onion and beansprouts. Full of protein and even gluten-free, this is one healthy midday munch.

Where: Cocu (Baggot Street), Cocu (Hatch Street), Cocu (Chatham Street)

3. Super Green Soup, The Lo-Cal Kitchen

A post shared by The Lo-cal Kitchen™ (@thelo_calkitchen) on Jan 26, 2017 at 2:27am PST

Giving any limp lunch-sandwich a run for its money, this mean green soup from Lo-Cal kitchen will have you building muscles like Popeye. Blended from all the best protein-rich greens, this steaming bowl has 7g of protein alone – plus you can order a side of chicken, eggs, tuna or smoked salmon to turn it into the ultimate muscle-maker.

Where: The Lo-Cal kitchen, Dublin 2

4. Sushi, Eatokyo

A post shared by Eatokyo (@eatokyo_dublin) on Apr 14, 2017 at 7:48am PDT

For something satisfying but sophisticated, you can't go wrong with sushi. Naturally good for you and with ingredients as fresh as they come, Eatokyo has some of the best options around. Start off with thinly sliced sashimi before moving on to their salmon and rocket rolls. You'll be back for seconds. And thirds – and a small platter encourage you to mix it up. Get a curated combination, or pick your own from the a la carte menu. We love the classic Salmon Avocado roll for all those healthy fats.

Where: Eatokyo, Temple Quay

5. Nut butter Toast, Póg

A post shared by Póg (@pog_dublin) on Nov 16, 2016 at 3:02pm PST

We couldn't resist adding something sweet, and this dish from Póg is the perfect weekend brunch. Naughty but nice, this treat still packs in 6.5g protein. Crisp toast is spread with natural nut-butter before being topped with sliced banana, dark chocolate and a drizzle of honey. Get one of your five-a-day the easy way.

Where: Póg, Dublin 1
